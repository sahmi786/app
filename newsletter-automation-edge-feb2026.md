The VMware Migration Reality Check

Everyone's talking about VMware migrations. Few are talking about why so many fail.

After helping dozens of organisations navigate the post-Broadcom landscape, here's what I've learned: the technical migration is the easy part. The hard part is the organisational change that comes with it.

Nutanix recently reported "hundreds" of VMware customers migrating in Q1 FY2026 alone. But the stock dipped. Why? Migration complexity is taking longer than expected. Revenue is shifting to later quarters because these projects are bigger and messier than anticipated.

This isn't a Nutanix problem - it's an industry reality.

The three migration killers I see most often:

1. Underestimating the app dependency mapping - You don't know what you don't know until you start moving things
2. Skills gaps - Your VMware experts need time to become AHV experts
3. The "lift and shift" trap - Moving without optimising just relocates technical debt

The organisations succeeding are the ones treating this as a transformation, not a migration.

We helped a retail client connect their infrastructure metrics to business outcomes. Now they can actually answer that question. That's the difference between monitoring and observability.

üîÆ TECH TRENDS TO WATCH

Here's what's on my radar for 2026:

1. Edge AI Goes Mainstream - AI chips in edge devices are enabling real-time decisions without cloud round-trips. Predictive maintenance, smart traffic, instant fraud detection - all happening at the edge. If your architecture is still cloud-only, you're already behind.

2. Kubernetes Becomes the AI Orchestrator - GPU-centric workloads are exploding, and K8s is evolving to handle them. Self-healing clusters, intelligent scheduling for expensive accelerator hardware - this is where the action is.

3. Multi-Cloud Stops Being Optional - It's no longer "should we go multi-cloud?" but "how do we manage it effectively?" The winners will be those with consistent tooling across providers.

4. FinOps Matures - Cloud cost management is finally getting serious. Expect more integration between infrastructure decisions and financial impact. The question "what does this deployment cost per transaction?" will become standard.

5. Platform Engineering Takes Centre Stage - Internal developer platforms are moving from "nice to have" to "competitive advantage." Reducing cognitive load on dev teams is the new productivity multiplier.

üìö WHAT I'M READING

"Team Topologies" by Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais

Re-reading this one. As infrastructure becomes more software-defined, the way we organise teams matters more than ever. The concept of "cognitive load" in platform engineering is something every infrastructure leader should understand.

Key insight: Your platform team's job isn't to build features. It's to reduce the cognitive load on stream-aligned teams. Changes how you measure success.
